Counties Around Atlanta Map: A Guide To Exploring Georgia

Introduction

If you’re planning a trip to Georgia, it’s important to know the different counties around Atlanta. The greater Atlanta area is home to several counties, each with its unique attractions and landmarks. In this article, we’ll explore the counties around Atlanta map and highlight the best places to visit and things to do in each county.

The Counties Around Atlanta Map

The counties around Atlanta include Fulton, DeKalb, Cobb, Gwinnett, Clayton, Cherokee, Henry, Douglas, Fayette, and Rockdale. Each county has its own culture, history, and attractions that make it worth exploring. Here’s a brief overview of each county:

Fulton County

Fulton County is home to the city of Atlanta and has several iconic landmarks, such as the Georgia Aquarium, World of Coca-Cola, and the Martin Luther King Jr. National Historic Site. It’s also home to several universities and museums, making it a great place for history and culture enthusiasts.

DeKalb County

DeKalb County is known for its vibrant music scene and outdoor attractions. It’s home to Stone Mountain Park, a popular destination for hiking and sightseeing, as well as the Fernbank Museum of Natural History and the Atlanta Symphony Orchestra.

Cobb County

Cobb County is home to several historic sites, such as the Kennesaw Mountain National Battlefield Park and the Marietta Square. It’s also home to Six Flags Over Georgia, making it a great destination for families with children.

Gwinnett County

Gwinnett County is known for its shopping and dining options, as well as its beautiful parks and recreation areas. It’s home to the Mall of Georgia, the largest shopping mall in the state, as well as several golf courses and parks.

Clayton County

Clayton County is home to several historic sites, such as the Stately Oaks Plantation and the Road to Tara Museum. It’s also home to Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport, making it a convenient destination for travelers.

Cherokee County

Cherokee County is known for its beautiful natural scenery and outdoor recreation opportunities. It’s home to Lake Allatoona, a popular destination for boating and fishing, as well as several hiking and biking trails.

Henry County

Henry County is home to several historic sites, such as the Nash Farm Battlefield and the Patrick Cleburne Confederate Cemetery. It’s also home to several shopping and dining options.

Douglas County

Douglas County is known for its beautiful parks and recreation areas, such as the Sweetwater Creek State Park and the Boundary Waters Aquatic Center. It’s also home to several historic sites, such as the Old Courthouse Museum.

Fayette County

Fayette County is known for its beautiful natural scenery and outdoor recreation opportunities. It’s home to several parks and hiking trails, as well as the Fayette Pavilion, a popular shopping and dining destination.

Rockdale County

Rockdale County is known for its historic sites and natural attractions, such as the Panola Mountain State Park and the Olde Town Conyers Historic District. It’s also home to several shopping and dining options.

FAQs

Q: What is the best county to visit around Atlanta?

A: It depends on your interests. If you’re interested in history and culture, Fulton and DeKalb counties are great choices. If you’re looking for outdoor recreation opportunities, Cherokee and Douglas counties are worth exploring. If you’re traveling with children, Cobb County is a great destination.

Q: What are some must-visit attractions in the counties around Atlanta?

A: Some of the must-visit attractions include the Georgia Aquarium, Stone Mountain Park, the Mall of Georgia, and the Panola Mountain State Park.

Q: What is the best time to visit the counties around Atlanta?

A: The best time to visit depends on your interests. If you’re looking for outdoor activities, spring and fall are great times to visit. If you’re interested in festivals and events, summer is a great time to visit.

Conclusion

The counties around Atlanta offer a diverse range of attractions and activities that make it a great destination for travelers. Whether you’re interested in history and culture, outdoor recreation, or shopping and dining, there’s something for everyone in the counties around Atlanta map. Plan your trip today and discover the beauty of Georgia!
