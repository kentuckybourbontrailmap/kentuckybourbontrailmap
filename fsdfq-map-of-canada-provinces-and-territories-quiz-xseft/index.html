Map of Canada Provinces and Territories Quiz: Test Your Knowledge Canada is the second-largest country in the world by land area, and it is divided into 13 provinces and territories. Each province and territory has its own unique features, history, and culture. If you are planning to visit Canada or just want to test your knowledge, take this quiz and see how well you know the map of Canada provinces and territories.

Provinces of Canada

Canada has ten provinces, each with its own capital city, flag, and coat of arms. The provinces are:

1. Newfoundland and Labrador

2. Prince Edward Island

3. Nova Scotia

4. New Brunswick

5. Quebec

6. Ontario

7. Manitoba

8. Saskatchewan

9. Alberta

10. British Columbia

Question 1: What is the capital city of Newfoundland and Labrador?

The capital city of Newfoundland and Labrador is St. John’s, which is located on the eastern coast of the province.

Question 2: Which province is known for its lobster and potatoes?

Prince Edward Island is known for its delicious lobster and potatoes. The island is also famous for its scenic beaches and red soil.

Question 3: What is the capital city of Ontario?

The capital city of Ontario is Toronto, which is the largest city in Canada and the fourth-largest city in North America.

Territories of Canada

Canada also has three territories, which are located in the northern part of the country. The territories are:

1. Yukon

2. Northwest Territories

3. Nunavut

Question 4: Which territory is the largest by land area?

Nunavut is the largest territory by land area, covering over 2 million square kilometers. It is also the least populated territory in Canada.

Question 5: Which territory is known for its gold rush history?

Yukon is known for its gold rush history, which began in the late 19th century. The territory is also famous for its beautiful natural scenery, including the Northern Lights.

Conclusion

How well did you do on the map of Canada provinces and territories quiz? If you got all the answers correct, congratulations! You are a true Canada expert. If you missed a few, don’t worry, there’s always room for improvement. Canada is a fascinating country with so much to explore, so why not plan a trip and see it for yourself?
