    The Geography of England and Wales

    England and Wales are located in the northwestern part of Europe. England is surrounded by the Irish Sea, the North Sea, and the English Channel. Wales, on the other hand, is bordered by the Irish Sea and the Bristol Channel. These countries are home to many beautiful cities, including London, Manchester, Cardiff, and Edinburgh.

    The History of England and Wales

    England and Wales have a rich and fascinating history. The earliest recorded history of these countries dates back to the Roman era. Over the centuries, England and Wales have been ruled by various kingdoms and empires, including the Anglo-Saxons, Vikings, and Normans. Today, England and Wales are part of the United Kingdom and have a constitutional monarchy.

    The Culture of England and Wales

    The culture of England and Wales is diverse and vibrant. These countries are known for their literature, music, art, and sports.     What is an Outline Map?

    An outline map is a map that shows only the boundaries of a country or region. It does not show any other details, such as cities, rivers, or mountains. An outline map is used to show the shape and size of a country or region.

    The Outline Map of England

    The outline map of England shows the shape and boundaries of England. England is the largest country in the United Kingdom and is located in the southern part of the island of Great Britain. The outline of England is rectangular in shape and is approximately 600 miles long and 300 miles wide.

    The Outline Map of Wales

    The outline map of Wales shows the shape and boundaries of Wales. Wales is located in the western part of the island of Great Britain and is bordered by England to the east. The outline of Wales is irregular in shape and is approximately 170 miles long and 60 miles wide.
