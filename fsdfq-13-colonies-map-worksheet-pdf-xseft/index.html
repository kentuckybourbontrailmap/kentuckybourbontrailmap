What are the 13 Colonies?

The 13 colonies were the British colonies that existed in North America in the 17th and 18th centuries. These colonies were divided into three regions: the New England Colonies, the Middle Colonies, and the Southern Colonies. Each colony had its own unique cultural and economic characteristics.

The New England Colonies

The New England Colonies were located in the northeastern part of the United States. They included Massachusetts, Connecticut, Rhode Island, and New Hampshire. These colonies were known for their shipbuilding, fishing, and trade.

Massachusetts

Massachusetts was founded in 1620 by the Pilgrims. It was known for its strong religious beliefs and played a key role in the American Revolution.

Connecticut

Connecticut was founded in 1636 by Thomas Hooker. It was known for its manufacturing and played an important role in the American Revolution.

Rhode Island

Rhode Island was founded in 1636 by Roger Williams. It was known for its religious tolerance and played an important role in the American Revolution.

New Hampshire

New Hampshire was founded in 1623 by John Mason. It was known for its fishing and played an important role in the American Revolution.

The Middle Colonies

The Middle Colonies were located in the middle of the eastern seaboard. They included New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, and Delaware. These colonies were known for their agriculture, trade, and diversity.

New York

New Jersey

Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania was founded in 1681 by William Penn. It was known for its religious tolerance and played a key role in the American Revolution.

Delaware

Delaware was founded in 1638 by the Swedish. It was known for its agriculture and played a key role in the American Revolution.

The Southern Colonies

The Southern Colonies were located in the southern part of the United States. They included Virginia, Maryland, North Carolina, South Carolina, and Georgia. These colonies were known for their agriculture, slavery, and plantation system.

Virginia

Maryland

Q&A

Q: Why were the New England Colonies known for shipbuilding, fishing, and trade?

A: The New England Colonies were located near the coast, which made them well-suited for shipbuilding and fishing. They also had access to important trade routes, which allowed them to become major players in the trade industry.

Q: What was the plantation system in the Southern Colonies?

A: The plantation system was a system of agriculture in which large plantations were run by wealthy landowners who used enslaved Africans to work the land. This system was prevalent in the Southern Colonies and played a key role in their economy.
