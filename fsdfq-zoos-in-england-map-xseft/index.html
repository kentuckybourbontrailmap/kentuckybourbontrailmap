Introduction

Are you a fan of animals? England has some of the best zoos in the world, which are perfect for animal lovers of all ages. In this article, we will guide you through the top zoos in England, and where to find them on the map. From London to Manchester, there is an array of zoos to choose from, each with its unique features and attractions. Whether you are looking for a family trip or a solo adventure, this guide is for you.

What are the Top Zoos in England?

There are many zoos in England, but the top zoos that you must visit are:

1. London Zoo

Located in Regent’s Park in London, the London Zoo is one of the oldest and most famous zoos in the world. It was opened in 1828, and it boasts a variety of animals, including lions, tigers, penguins, and gorillas. The zoo is also home to over 750 species of animals.

2. Chester Zoo

The Chester Zoo is one of the most popular zoos in England. It is located in Chester, and it is home to over 35,000 animals. The zoo is also known for its conservation efforts and research programs.

3. Bristol Zoo Gardens

The Bristol Zoo Gardens is located in Bristol, and it is home to over 700 species of animals. It is also known for its botanical gardens and conservation programs.

What are the Best Things to Do in these Zoos?

Each zoo has its unique features and attractions that you must see. Here are some of the best things to do in these zoos:

1. London Zoo

Visit the Land of the Lions exhibit, which is home to Asiatic lions. The exhibit is designed to look like a village in India, and it is an immersive experience for visitors.

2. Chester Zoo

Visit the Islands exhibit, which is home to exotic animals such as Sumatran tigers and orangutans. The exhibit is designed to replicate the natural habitats of these animals.

3. Bristol Zoo Gardens

Visit the Twilight World exhibit, which is home to nocturnal animals such as bats, sloths, and owls. The exhibit is designed to replicate the animals’ natural habitats at night.
